Why your smart thermostat isn't actually saving you money yet

8 min read · Updated June 2026 · By the Stackly Smart Home team

Buying a smart thermostat and leaving it on its default schedule is a bit like buying a bike and only ever riding it in first gear. It works. It's just not doing the thing you paid for.

Start with the setback, not the schedule

Most people set one schedule and stop there. The bigger gains come from the setback temperature — how far the system lets the room drift while you're out — not from adding more time blocks. A two-degree setback during work hours typically costs you nothing in comfort and shows up clearly on the next bill.

Let it learn before you fight it

Adaptive thermostats need real data, not a perfect schedule from day one. Resist the urge to override it constantly in the first two weeks. Every manual override is a data point telling the system its guess was wrong, so a noisy first week actually speeds up how fast it converges on your real routine.

Check the recovery setting once

"Smart recovery" decides how early the system starts heating or cooling before you're due home, so the room is at temperature exactly when you walk in rather than starting cold. It's usually off by default. Turning it on is one toggle and is the single biggest comfort-per-dollar setting most people never find.

The four settings worth checking this week

  • Setback temperature for unoccupied hours
  • Smart recovery / pre-conditioning
  • Per-room scheduling, if your system supports zones
  • Humidity-linked adjustments, which most people never touch

None of this requires new hardware. It's twenty minutes in the app, and it's the difference between owning a smart thermostat and just owning a thermostat with a nicer screen.
